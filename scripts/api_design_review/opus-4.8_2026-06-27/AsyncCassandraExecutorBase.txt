1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
In very good shape. Every synchronous operation in CassandraExecutorBase has an async
counterpart here, and the wrapping is consistent end to end: single-row reads preserve
the sync Optional/Nullable contract (get->Optional, gett->nullable T, findFirst->Optional,
queryForSingleValue/queryForString/queryForDate->Nullable, queryForSingleNonNull->Optional,
queryForBoolean/...->primitive Optional*), multi-row reads return List/Dataset/Stream, and
mutations return the driver RS — each uniformly wrapped in ContinuableFuture. Deprecation
parity is correct (count(String,Object...) deprecated on both sync and async ->
queryForLong). The sync() accessor is appropriately typed and instances correctly
originate from sync.async().

5. MISSING METHODS
[1] queryForDate raw-query form: ContinuableFuture<Nullable<Date>> queryForDate(String query,
    Object...) | AsyncCassandraExecutorBase.java:2254, 2292 (only Condition forms exist) | Low |
    Every other scalar queryForXxx exposes BOTH a (targetClass,propName,Condition) form and a
    raw (String,Object...) form (e.g. queryForString(String,Object...)@2711,
    queryForLong(String,Object...)@2599); only queryForDate lacks the raw-query form. This is
    mirrored identically in the sync base (queryForDate@1921/1957 only), so it is a library-
    wide gap, not async-specific, and queryForSingleValue(Date.class,query,..) substitutes.
    Recommendation: for symmetry add queryForDate(String,Object...) delegating to
    queryForSingleValue(Date.class,...), or document that path. Tracked jointly with
    CassandraExecutorBase 5[1].
