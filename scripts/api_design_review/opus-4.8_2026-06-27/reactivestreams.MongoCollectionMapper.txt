1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
A faithful Publisher-returning mirror of the sync mapper and itself a thin pre-typed delegate
(every method one-lines to the reactive collectionExecutor, no per-call Class<T>). Method-
for-method parity with the sync mapper is essentially complete; the only intentional, correct
omissions are the gett(...) family (absence is conveyed by empty Mono) and the stream(...)
family (the cold Flux from list(...) IS the streaming form). Return shapes are well chosen
and, notably, MORE internally consistent than the sync class for single-entity reads — get,
findFirst, and all findOneAndX uniformly return Mono<T> (empty == absent). The findings are
the shared @Beta inconsistencies plus one Javadoc-accuracy divergence from the sync mirror.

2. NAMING ISSUES — None.

3. RETURN-TYPE AND PARAMETER ISSUES
[1] Collection<? extends T> update overloads documented as a "merge from multiple sources"
    rather than the aggregation-pipeline updates they actually are: updateOne(Bson,Collection)
    @2194 ("partial updates from multiple sources"), updateMany(Bson,Collection)@2302,
    findOneAndUpdate(Bson,Collection)@2815 ("aggregated from various entities") |
    reactivestreams/MongoCollectionMapper.java (cited lines) | Low | The sync mirror documents
    these identical methods correctly as pipeline-stage updates (sync @2250-2256, 2375-2381).
    The reactive wording can mislead a caller into expecting last-write-wins field merging
    rather than a $-pipeline. Per ground rule 5, correct the misleading docs rather than excuse
    them. Recommendation: copy the sync "each item becomes a pipeline stage" description onto
    these reactive overloads (no signature change).
