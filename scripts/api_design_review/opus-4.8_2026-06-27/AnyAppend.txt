1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
Solid wrapper over org.apache.hadoop.hbase.client.Append exposing the expected
append-specific controls (setTimeRange/getTimeRange, setReturnResults/isReturnResults),
addColumn(String,String,Object) / addColumn(byte[],byte[],byte[]), add(Cell), factories
and val(). Its add-value shape matches AnyIncrement. The actionable items are all
cross-class consistency issues shared with the other three mutation types: a checked-
exception divergence on add(Cell), a redundant setAttribute overload, and a row-key
parameter-type asymmetry in the slice/familyMap factories.

2. NAMING ISSUES
None.

3. RETURN-TYPE AND PARAMETER ISSUES
[1] add(Cell) | AnyAppend.java:605 | Med | add(Cell) here does NOT declare `throws
    IOException`, whereas all three siblings DO: AnyPut.add(Cell)@AnyPut.java:1370,
    AnyDelete.add(Cell)@AnyDelete.java:520, AnyIncrement.add(Cell)@AnyIncrement.java:356.
    VERIFIED against source. The signature divergence is the actionable defect (it
    mirrors HBase Append.add not throwing, but inconsistency across the wrapper family is
    the issue). The agent's stronger "silently drops a row-mismatched cell" framing is an
    HBase-internal behavior not visible in this file — treat that as Low/unverified.
    Recommendation: align the family — either add validation that throws on row mismatch
    here, or accept the divergence and document why only Append differs.
[2] setAttribute(String,byte[]) | AnyAppend.java:636 | Med | Redundant with the inherited
    setAttribute(String,Object)@AnyOperationWithAttributes.java:143 — its own Javadoc
    (lines 616-619) admits it is "observationally equivalent" (toValueBytes returns byte[]
    as-is) — and present on only 2 of 4 mutation types (also AnyIncrement.java:700; absent
    on AnyPut/AnyDelete). VERIFIED via grep across the hbase package. Recommendation:
    remove from AnyAppend and AnyIncrement for consistency (or add to AnyPut/AnyDelete).
    One finding spanning AnyAppend, AnyIncrement, AnyPut, AnyDelete.
[3] of(byte[],int,int) | AnyAppend.java:269 ; of(byte[],long,NavigableMap) | AnyAppend.java:301
    | Med | These factories accept only byte[] row keys, whereas the corresponding
    AnyPut.of(Object,int,int)@AnyPut.java:370 and AnyDelete.of(Object,...)@AnyDelete.java:344/423
    accept Object (converting via toRowBytes). Recommendation: add Object-keyed overloads
    to AnyAppend (and AnyIncrement) so the row-key contract is uniform across all four
    mutation types.
