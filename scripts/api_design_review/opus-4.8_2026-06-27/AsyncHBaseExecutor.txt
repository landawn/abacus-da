1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
Sync<->async parity is excellent. Every synchronous operation (exists/get/scan/put/delete/
mutateRow/append/increment/incrementColumnValue/coprocessorService/batchCoprocessorService,
including all incrementColumnValue and all 12 scan overloads) has an async counterpart with
the same parameter list and a ContinuableFuture-of-the-sync-return shape; void ops correctly
become ContinuableFuture<Void>. Non-operational members (admin/connection/getTable/mapper/
static converters) are intentionally reachable only via sync(), as documented. Notably the
async coprocessor-with-callback/batch methods do NOT leak `throws Exception` (deferred to
get()), which is cleaner than their sync counterparts (see HBaseExecutor 3[2]).
