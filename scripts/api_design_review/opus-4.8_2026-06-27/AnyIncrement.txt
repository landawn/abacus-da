1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
Clean mirror of org.apache.hadoop.hbase.client.Increment with the same fluent shape as
AnyAppend: addColumn(String,String,long) / addColumn(byte[],byte[],long), add(Cell),
setTimeRange/getTimeRange, setReturnResults/isReturnResults, plus the Increment-specific
hasFamilies() and getFamilyMapOfLongs(). add(Cell) correctly throws IOException
(consistent with Put/Delete, unlike Append). The actionable items are the same cross-
cutting consistency issues as AnyAppend.

2. NAMING ISSUES
None.

3. RETURN-TYPE AND PARAMETER ISSUES
[1] setAttribute(String,byte[]) | AnyIncrement.java:700 | Med | Same redundant/inconsistent
    overload as AnyAppend.setAttribute(String,byte[])@636 — observationally equivalent to
    the inherited Object overload and present on only 2 of 4 mutation types. VERIFIED.
    Recommendation: resolve uniformly across the family (remove from AnyAppend/AnyIncrement,
    or add to AnyPut/AnyDelete). See AnyAppend 3[2].
[2] of(byte[],int,int) | AnyIncrement.java:225 ; of(byte[],long,NavigableMap) | AnyIncrement.java:262
    | Med | Same row-key-type asymmetry as AnyAppend 3[3]: only byte[] accepted vs Object on
    the AnyPut/AnyDelete equivalents. Recommendation: add Object-keyed overloads for a
    uniform factory contract.
