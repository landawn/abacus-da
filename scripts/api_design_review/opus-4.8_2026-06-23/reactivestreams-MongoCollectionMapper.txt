1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT
A faithful reactive mirror of the blocking MongoCollectionMapper<T>: single->Mono<T>, multi->Flux<T>,
scalar->Mono<wrapper>, query->Mono<DataSet>, writes->Mono<Result>; parameter ordering matches and
cold-publisher semantics are documented throughout. The one substantive issue is a behavioral divergence
in distinct(); the rest are an accessor-name drift and inherent Mono conventions.

2. NAMING ISSUES
- [RESOLVED 2026-06-24 — code-fixed] collectionExecutor() | reactivestreams/MongoCollectionMapper.java:220
  | Medium | Drifted from the blocking sibling's mongoCollectionExecutor(). FIXED: the BLOCKING mapper was
  hard-renamed mongoCollectionExecutor() -> collectionExecutor() to match this reactive accessor (no
  alias; blocking javadoc @see/@link refs + test updated); this reactive method is unchanged. Both now use
  collectionExecutor(). test-compile green.

3. RETURN-TYPE AND PARAMETER ISSUES
- [RESOLVED 2026-06-24 — code-fixed] distinct(String) / distinct(String,Bson) |
  reactivestreams/MongoCollectionMapper.java:3020,3077 | Medium | BEHAVIORAL DIVERGENCE from the blocking
  twin (the only real behavioral defect in the review). Was: the blocking mapper routes scalar distinct
  through a $group/$project pipeline (private distinctPipeline) because the driver's native
  distinct(field, entityClass) throws BsonInvalidOperationException on scalar fields, but the reactive
  mapper delegated to native distinct — so userMapper.distinct("country") succeeded blocking, errored
  reactively. FIXED: both reactive distinct(...) now route through collectionExecutor.aggregate(
  distinctPipeline(...), rowType) with a private distinctPipeline helper copied from the blocking mapper,
  plus N.checkArgNotEmpty/checkArgNotNull validation and corrected Javadoc.
  NOTE — fix placement corrected: the original write-up suggested "fix in the reactive executor.distinct",
  but that was wrong: the blocking workaround lives in the MAPPER (the blocking executor.distinct is
  native), and pipelining the reactive executor.distinct(field, valueClass) would break its scalar
  escape-hatch (collectionExecutor().distinct("department", String.class)) and diverge from the blocking
  executor. The reactive executor.distinct was therefore left UNCHANGED.
- queryForString/queryForDate/queryForSingleValue return Mono<...>, collapsing the blocking Nullable<...>
  present-but-null distinction | :1465,1512,1560,1609 | Low (by design) | Intrinsic to Mono; documented.
  Recorded so it isn't re-raised.
